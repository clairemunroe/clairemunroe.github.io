SARS-CoV-2 and Pregnancy: Protect Yourself and Your Child

SARS-CoV-2

SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19) belongs to the coronavirus family of viruses, named for the structure their spike proteins form. It is categorized as a severe acute respiratory syndrome with symptoms which include:

  • fever/chills

  • sore throat/cough

  • shortness of breathe

  • fatigue

Who is at higher risk?

People with pre-existing health conditions and those over the age of 65 are at higher risk of developing more severe symptoms of COVID-19. However, those who are pregnant are also considered high risk.

1 in 5 pregnant people diagnosed with COVID-19 need to be hospitalized.

How does COVID-19 impact pregnancy?

The mechanism in which COVID-19 infects cells is through ACE2 receptors whose levels are increased in the uterus and placenta. When the virus binds to these receptors in the placenta

  • bloodflow to the fetus can be restricted

  • inflammation in the tissues can cause damage

Damage to the placenta, which is there to protect the fetus, can then lead to

  • miscarriage

  • stillbirth

  • preterm labor

  • preaclampsia

  • hypoxia

Prevention and Treatment

The best preventative measure for pregnant people is to get the COVID-19 vaccine. It can protect mothers from severe symptoms and if taken during pregnancy, can provide the fetus with those antibodies as well. Other protections include:

  • Avoiding crowds and indoor spaces

  • Wearing a mask

  • Washing your hands regularly

Take the measures needed to protect yourself and your child!
